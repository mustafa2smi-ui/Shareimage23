What is the ruling on listening to music in Islam?

The ruling on music in Islam is a topic that has been discussed by scholars throughout Islamic history. There are differing opinions among the schools of thought.

The majority of classical scholars consider musical instruments to be prohibited (haram), with the exception of the duff (a simple frame drum) in specific circumstances like weddings.

This opinion is based on several hadiths, including the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) saying: "From among my followers there will be some people who will consider illegal sexual intercourse, the wearing of silk, the drinking of alcoholic drinks and the use of musical instruments, as lawful." (Sahih al-Bukhari)

However, some contemporary scholars make distinctions based on content, context, and intent, allowing certain forms of music that do not promote un-Islamic values.

Reference:

Sahih al-Bukhari 5590, Sunan Abu Dawud 4926, Al-Muwatta 1597
